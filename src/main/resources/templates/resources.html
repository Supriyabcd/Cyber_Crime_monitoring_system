Educational Resources — Cybercrime Case Management

Concise, actionable learning for common domains: Instagram Trolling, Hacking, Email Threats, and Game Bullying.

Legal resources

Understand the legal framework, reporting channels and what evidence to collect when a cyber incident happens.
  • Know which laws apply in your country (cybercrime, harassment, data protection).
  • Preserve evidence: screenshots, timestamps, exported chat logs, IP headers when available.
  • Report to the correct body — police cyber cell, platform abuse reporting, or national CERT.
Example (Instagram Trolling): Keep screenshots of abusive posts, note usernames, and URLs. Use platform's report flow and file a cyber complaint with authorities if threats or doxxing occur.

Threat alerts & advisories

Short, timely notices about active campaigns, phishing waves, credential leaks and trending scams.
  • Subscribe to platform advisories (CERT, platform status pages).
  • Watch for suspicious account takeover patterns.
  • Verify advisories via official sources before acting.
Example (Email Threats): A targeted phishing email claiming account suspension — verify sender domain, hover links, and contact official support (not reply-to address).

Identity protection guide

Steps to protect personal identity and recover from exposure or doxxing.
  • Use unique passwords and password managers; enable MFA.
  • Minimize public personal data; remove exposed PII.
  • If doxxed, document evidence, request takedowns, and notify authorities if financial info exposed.
Example (Hacking / Account Takeover): Revoke active sessions, change passwords, enable MFA, and check connected apps for malicious access.

Cybersecurity best practices

Practical day-to-day measures individuals and small teams can adopt to improve security posture.
  • Keep devices/software up-to-date; apply security patches regularly.
  • Think before you click: verify links, attachments, social engineering attempts.
  • Back up important data securely and test restores occasionally.
Example (Game Bullying): Enable privacy to hide friend lists, report/block abusers, collect chat logs for evidence, and consider parental controls for minors.
